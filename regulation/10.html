Regulation 10

Protecting Realm Hosters

Ensuring fairness, accountability, and network integrity for all designated Realm Hosters operating under the VSH brand.

All Realms and Discord servers under the Void Server Hub (VSH) Network remain the property of VSH. To ensure fairness, accountability, and network integrity, the following regulations apply to all designated Realm Hosters operating under the VSH brand.

1

Definition and Ownership

  • A Realm Hoster is the individual designated by VSH to operate and manage a server on behalf of the network.
  • While VSH retains full ownership of all servers, the Hoster maintains protected authority over daily operations and community management.
2

Authority & Accountability

  • Regardless of permissions or symbolic ownership roles, no member may override, remove, or restrict the Hoster's operational authority within their assigned server.
  • Hosters must comply with all Realm Hoster status requirements and network policies. Non-compliance may affect eligibility, benefits, or affiliation status.
3

Conditions for Removal

A Realm Hoster may only be removed or replaced under the following conditions:

  • Voluntary Departure – The Hoster explicitly agrees to step down.
  • Proven Damage – The Hoster deliberately and knowingly causes significant harm to their server (e.g., nuking, sabotage, data wipes, or exposure of vulnerabilities), supported by verified evidence reviewed by VSH Management.
  • Violation Clause – The Hoster is found in violation of a Level 4 or Level 5 offense under the Violation Classification System.
  • Inactivity Clause – If a Hoster is inactive for 1 consecutive month without notice, a temporary caretaker may be assigned. After 3 consecutive months of total inactivity, VSH reserves the right to reassign the Hoster role permanently, while still recognizing prior contributions.
4

Fair Treatment Clause

  • All parties—including Realm Hosters—will be treated with fairness, respect, and neutrality during any investigation, reassessment, or disciplinary process.
  • However, due to the dynamics of controlling a server, VSH retains final authority to act in the network's best interest when removal, reassessment, or caretaker actions are necessary.
5

Transparency & Notification Rights

  • Realm Hosters have the right to be informed of any accusations made against them, including the identity of the accuser unless confidentiality is required for safety.
  • The accuser will be informed that the Hoster will be notified of their claim.
  • Both parties may present their statements or evidence to VSH Management for review.
6

Evidence Handling Protocol

  • All evidence must be collected legitimately, transparently, and submitted privately to VSH Management.
  • Evidence and communications will be logged internally to maintain accountability, prevent tampering, and ensure procedural fairness.
7

Misconduct & False Report Safeguards

  • All reports are treated equally and reviewed objectively.
  • If an accuser or involved party is found to have made a false, retaliatory, or malicious report intended to damage VSH, a Realm Hoster, or any other individual, VSH may impose disciplinary action on the accuser or anyone complicit.
  • Weaponizing the reporting system is considered a serious offense under network policy.
8

Data & Asset Continuity

  • All server data, assets, permissions, and access credentials remain under VSH ownership at all times.
  • Hosters may not delete, withhold, or transfer assets without authorization from VSH Management.
  • Upon reassignment, all administrative control must be returned to VSH or the designated successor immediately.
9

Quarterly Re-Assessment

  • Each Realm Hoster will undergo a quarterly review conducted by VSH Management to evaluate compliance, performance, and overall server stability.
  • These reviews determine eligibility for continued operation, benefit tiers, or reassignment.
10

Appeal System

  • A Realm Hoster may appeal disciplinary or reassignment actions within 7 days of receiving notice.
  • Appeals must include counter-evidence or a written explanation.
  • During the appeal process, caretaker management remains in place, but Hoster authority is not permanently revoked until a final decision is made.
11

Proxy / Backup Hoster Designation

  • A Realm Hoster may designate a trusted backup operator, approved by VSH, to maintain server operations during absences or emergencies.
  • The backup's authority is temporary and expires upon the Hoster's return unless otherwise stated by VSH.
12

Conflict of Interest Clause

  • Realm Hosters may not operate, assist, or affiliate with competing Realm or Discord networks that conflict with VSH interests without prior written approval.
  • Any discovered conflict of interest may result in reassessment or removal from the Hoster position.
13

Enforcement & Oversight

  • VSH reserves the right to intervene, reassign, or act in any situation where network stability, integrity, or reputation is at risk.
  • All actions taken under this regulation will be documented and conducted through proper VSH channels.
  • Replacements, reassignments, or disciplinary actions under this regulation are final unless overturned through the appeal process.

Questions About Realm Hosting?

Contact our management team for clarification on any aspect of Regulation 10 or to discuss your role as a Realm Hoster.
